What is the point of a blog in 2026? Everyone posts in walled gardens now - LinkedIn, Substack, Facebook and so on. If you post there you can get natural reach. Maybe you'll be 'discovered' and you can build a audience you can eventually sell supplements to.

An audience is important. That's an understatement. An audience is life or death for a writer. Its feedback and growth and serendipity and bragging rights and connection and everything else.

It's also pretty intimidating.

If you are just learning then you don't want any of that. If you are finding your voice you need to grind out a few reps before you show it off. Even when you reach your full power you may have some ideas you are a bit unsure of. You don't want your coworkers and family to have it delivered to their feed. A blog lets you work in semi privacy, plus also lets you pick and choose your highlights to show the world.

If someone follows this blog directly, I love that (and probably I'll see you for Christmas), but most likely I'll use this as a first edition and cater my message for specific audiences.

My goal is to shape my thoughts on the world. Nothing pretentious, just a bit of focus. I want to take that random cloud of idea fragments we all have and turn it into a digestable narrative. It could be right or it could be wrong but critically, it will exist.
Then the space it occupied in my brain is free for the next thing or better yet stay empty for a few hours to go enjoy a movie instead.
